Why I'm Switching Salons

A meditation on small betrayals, chemical burns, and the particular grief of losing a hairdresser you loved.

By Yacob Reyes  ·  June 14, 2025  ·  4 min read

The manicurist leaned in close and said, "We can put on a topcoat with glitter." She had noticed I liked attention, which felt either like intimacy or surveillance, and I was still deciding which. There is a particular vulnerability in salons — the chemical smell, the cape, the mirror you can't escape — that makes you say things you wouldn't say anywhere else.
